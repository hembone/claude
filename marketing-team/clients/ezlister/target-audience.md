# Target Audience

## Primary Persona

### "The Scaling Seller"
**Name archetype:** Rachel, 31
**Who she is:** A creative entrepreneur running an Etsy shop that's outgrowing her current workflow. She started selling print-on-demand art, handmade jewelry, or vintage finds as a side hustle, and now she has 100–500 listings. She's spending more time cropping photos, making mockups, and managing listings than actually creating products. She uses 3–4 separate free tools to get everything done and is constantly switching tabs. She wants to scale without hiring help or burning out.

**Where she hangs out online:** YouTube (Etsy seller tutorials, shop reviews), TikTok (seller tips, shop day-in-my-life), Reddit (r/EtsySellers, r/Etsy), Facebook groups (Etsy seller communities), Etsy forums, Pinterest (for product research and mockup inspiration)

**What she's looking for:** A tool that consolidates her listing workflow — photo editing, mockups, and listing management in one place. It needs to handle bulk operations, work specifically with Etsy, and not cost a fortune while she's still growing.

**What wins her over:** Seeing the tool in action (a 60-second demo showing 50 photos cropped and exported). A free tier that lets her try it with real work before committing. Clear, specific feature descriptions — not vague marketing promises.

## Secondary Persona

### "The New-to-Serious Seller"
**Name archetype:** Marcus, 27
**Who he is:** Just crossed 50 sales and is realizing that manual processes don't scale. Still figuring out his workflow, experimenting with listing strategies, and looking for tools that help him look more professional. More price-sensitive — needs the free tier to get started. Watches a lot of YouTube tutorials and follows Etsy seller accounts on TikTok for tips.

## Pain Points

- **"I spend more time editing photos than making products."** Resizing, cropping, and formatting listing photos eats hours every week — especially when Etsy has specific aspect ratio requirements.
- **"Etsy's built-in tools are terrible for bulk work."** Editing one listing at a time through Etsy's dashboard is painfully slow when you have hundreds of listings.
- **"I use five different apps and nothing talks to each other."** A separate crop tool, a separate mockup app, a separate image host, plus Etsy's own dashboard. Every new listing means switching between all of them.
- **"I need mockups but Photoshop is overkill and Canva mockups look fake."** Getting realistic product mockups shouldn't require a design degree or an expensive subscription.
- **"I can't afford expensive seller tools on top of Etsy fees."** Between Etsy's listing fees, transaction fees, and ad costs, another $30/month subscription is a hard sell.
- **"I want to grow my shop but I'm already maxed out on time."** The bottleneck isn't ideas or products — it's the operational overhead of running the shop.

## Buying Motivations

- **Time savings** — The core driver. Reclaim hours per week that currently go to manual photo editing and listing management.
- **Professionalism** — Better photos and mockups directly translate to more clicks and more sales. Looking polished matters on Etsy.
- **Consolidation** — Fewer tools, fewer tabs, fewer logins. One place for the visual and operational side of running a shop.
- **Scalability** — Handle 500 listings as easily as 50. Grow without the workflow breaking down.
- **Low risk** — A free tier means trying the tool on real work before spending anything. No commitment, no credit card upfront.
- **Competitive edge** — Look as polished as top sellers without their budget or team. Level the playing field.
