# Content Guidelines

## Approved Topics

- Etsy listing photo best practices (sizing, lighting, aspect ratios, backgrounds)
- Product mockup creation tutorials and tips
- Batch workflow efficiency for Etsy sellers
- Etsy SEO basics as context for why good listing photos matter
- Seller success stories and case studies (with permission)
- Etsy policy updates that affect listing requirements or photo specs
- Seasonal selling strategies and holiday prep timelines
- Print-on-demand workflow tips and tools
- Shop organization and inventory management strategies
- Before/after listing transformations using ezListr tools
- Etsy algorithm updates and how they affect visibility
- Comparisons of seller tools and workflows (honest, not attack-style)

## Content Pillars

### 1. Tool Tutorials
How-to content for each of the four tools — Cropper, Mockup Builder, Gallery, and Listings Manager. Screen recordings, step-by-step guides, tips and tricks. This is the core content type and the primary reason people discover ezListr through search and social.

### 2. Seller Productivity
Time-saving strategies, workflow optimization, batch processing techniques, and "work smarter" content. Positions ezListr as a calm efficiency brand. Appeals to sellers who feel overwhelmed by operational overhead. Tone: calm and practical, never hustle-coded.

### 3. Etsy Seller Education
Listing photo best practices, what makes a good mockup, how top sellers organize their shops, Etsy SEO fundamentals. Builds authority and attracts the target audience through search and shares — even if they don't use ezListr yet.

### 4. Product Updates
New features, improvements, roadmap previews, and changelogs. Keeps existing users engaged, shows momentum, and signals that the tool is actively developed and improving.

## Visual Style for Content

All content graphics and templates should reflect the ezListr visual identity:

- **Backgrounds:** Warm paper tones (`#faf7f2`, `#f2ece1`) — never cold gray or pure white
- **Accent color:** Terracotta (`#d4704a`) for highlights, callouts, and emphasis text
- **Typography:** Instrument Serif for display text and headers in graphics; Geist for body and labels; JetBrains Mono for technical callouts and labels
- **Texture:** Subtle paper grain overlays on marketing graphics where appropriate — consistent with the brand's tactile, calm aesthetic
- **UI screenshots:** Shown on warm paper backgrounds. The tool UI is always the hero.
- **No cold blue, navy, or stark white backgrounds** in branded graphics

## Platform-Specific Rules

### YouTube
- **Format:** 5–15 minute tutorials ("How to batch crop 100 Etsy photos in 2 minutes"), seller tip compilations, full tool walkthroughs, workflow comparisons
- **Thumbnails:** Warm paper background, terracotta for emphasis text, Instrument Serif or Geist for headline. UI screenshot as the central visual. Calm, readable at a glance.
- **Tone:** Helpful and direct. Get to the value fast — no 90-second intros. "Here's the problem, here's the solution, here's how."
- **Descriptions:** Keyword-rich, timestamped, with links to relevant tools and related videos
- **Posting cadence:** 1–2 videos/week

### TikTok / Reels / Shorts
- **Format:** 15–60 second quick tips, before/after transformations, "watch me list a product in 30 seconds" speed demos, seller pain point setups
- **Tone:** Casual and relatable, seller-to-seller. Show the real frustration of manual workflows, then the calm fix. Dry wit works here.
- **Text on screen:** Brief, readable, clean Geist or sans-serif. Warm background where possible. Never more than two lines.
- **Hook:** Lead with the pain point or the result — never the tool name. "POV: you have 50 photos to crop for Etsy" not "Check out ezListr's crop tool."
- **Posting cadence:** 3–5/week

### Reddit
- **Subreddits:** r/EtsySellers, r/Etsy, r/printondemand, r/ecommerce
- **Approach:** Participate authentically. Share genuine tips, answer questions, contribute to discussions. Link to ezListr only when directly relevant and helpful — never hard-sell.
- **Tone:** Peer-to-peer. No marketing voice. Write like a seller who found a useful tool, not like the tool's marketing team.
- **Posting cadence:** Daily engagement in comments, 1–2 original posts/week (value-first, not promotional)

### Instagram
- **Format:** Carousel tips (5–7 slides), Reels (cross-posted from TikTok), tool screenshot walkthroughs, seller tips as clean branded graphics
- **Grid aesthetic:** Warm paper backgrounds, terracotta accents, Instrument Serif for display text, UI screenshots as the hero. Recognizable at a glance — someone should spot an ezListr post by the warm paper + terracotta pattern.
- **Captions:** Short and specific for product posts. Longer and educational for tips. End with a question or soft CTA. No "LINK IN BIO!!!" — one calm sentence if needed.
- **Stories:** Quick polls ("biggest listing day pain point?"), feature teasers, behind-the-scenes, user shoutouts
- **Hashtags:** 10–15 per post. Mix of niche (#etsyseller #etsytips #listingphotos #productmockups) and broader (#smallbusiness #ecommerce #makerlife)
- **Posting cadence:** 3–4 feed posts/week, daily stories when active

### Email
- **Tone:** Helpful and concise — like a quick tip from a fellow seller. Calm, no urgency pressure.
- **Format:** One clear message per email. Feature announcements, tip of the week, or seller spotlights. Image-led with a single CTA.
- **Subject lines:** Direct and benefit-led. "Crop 50 photos in 60 seconds — here's how" not "ezListr Newsletter #14"
- **Cadence:** 1/week max. Every email should earn the open.

### Blog / SEO
- **Tone:** Informative, practical, and visually rich with screenshots
- **Topics:** Long-form guides targeting search — "best Etsy listing photo size 2026," "how to make product mockups for Etsy," "Etsy listing management tools compared"
- **Format:** 800–1,500 words with multiple screenshots and step-by-step sections. Scannable with headers and short paragraphs.
- **SEO focus:** Target long-tail keywords like "batch crop photos for Etsy," "free Etsy mockup tool," "how to manage Etsy listings in bulk," "Etsy listing photo requirements"
- **Cadence:** 2–4 posts/month
