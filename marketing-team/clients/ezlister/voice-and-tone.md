# Voice and Tone

## Writing Style

ezListr writes like a knowledgeable fellow seller — someone who's been in the trenches, knows the shortcuts, and shares them without ego. The voice is **clear, confident, and practical** with a friendly, encouraging undertone.

**Key principles:**
- **Get to the point** — Sellers are busy. Respect their time. Lead with the benefit, skip the fluff.
- **Be specific** — Name exact features and outcomes. "Batch crop 50 photos in under a minute" beats "save time on your photos."
- **Talk like a peer** — We understand Etsy because we build for it every day. No lecturing, no talking down.
- **Helpful over clever** — Clarity beats wordplay every time. If a sentence needs re-reading, rewrite it.

**Example tones:**
- Feature announcement: "Batch crop 50 listing photos in under a minute. Export as ZIP, upload to Etsy."
- Tutorial: "Your mockup is almost done — drag to adjust placement, then hit Export."
- Social post: "Stop resizing photos one by one. There's a faster way."

## Tone

| Dimension | Setting |
|-----------|---------|
| Formality | Casual-professional. Contractions are fine. Slang is not. |
| Humor | Light and relatable. Etsy seller in-jokes are welcome. Memes sparingly. |
| Energy | Upbeat but grounded. Enthusiastic about solving problems, never hype-y. |
| Warmth | Medium-high. Supportive and encouraging without being gushy. |
| Authority | Strong. We know Etsy seller workflows inside and out. |

**Tone shifts by context:**
- **Product UI:** Ultra-concise and action-oriented. Labels, tooltips, and confirmation messages — no extra words.
- **Marketing pages:** Benefit-led with slightly more personality. Show what the tool does and why it matters.
- **Tutorials:** Patient and step-by-step. No assumptions about skill level. Screenshots and screen recordings over paragraphs.
- **Support:** Empathetic and solution-focused. Acknowledge the frustration, then fix the problem.

## Vocabulary Preferences

**Words we use:**
- Toolkit, workflow, batch, streamline, manage
- Listing, mockup, crop, export, upload
- Etsy shop, sellers, listings, photos
- Save time, in one place, bulk, organize

**Words we lean into for texture:**
- "Serious sellers" (as in: "built for sellers who take their shop seriously")
- "One place" (as in: "crop, mockup, and manage — all in one place")
- "In bulk" (as in: "edit listings in bulk, not one by one")
- "Listing day" (as in: "make listing day your fastest day of the week")

## Words and Phrases to Avoid

| Avoid | Why | Use instead |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| "Revolutionary" / "game-changing" | Overpromise. Every SaaS tool claims this. | "Faster," "simpler," "streamlined" |
| "Hustle" / "grind" | Toxic productivity culture — sellers want less of that, not more | "Grow your shop," "work smarter" |
| "Easy-peasy" / "super simple" | Cutesy and dismissive | "Straightforward," "quick," "takes seconds" |
| "Cheap" / "budget" | Devalues the product and the seller | "Free to start," "affordable" |
| "Synergy" / "leverage" / "optimize" | Corporate jargon that sounds like a pitch deck | Plain language — "use," "improve," "get more from" |
| "All-in-one" (used alone) | Vague and overused | Name the specific tools: "crop, mockup, gallery, and listings" |
| "Limited time offer!" / "Act now!" | High-pressure sales tactics don't fit | "Now available," "just shipped" |
| Heavy emoji use | Clutters the clean, professional tone | Sparingly — one relevant emoji max per post |
