# Visual Identity

## Color Palette

A warm neutral palette rooted in natural materials — cream, clay, sage, and stone. Feels like a sunlit room with linen curtains and wooden frames. Aligned with the 2026 trend toward "emotional neutrals" — warm, skin-tinted, mineral-inspired tones replacing cool grays.

### Primary Colors

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| **Warm Ivory** | `#F5F0E8` | Primary background. Shop banners, website, social templates. |
| **Soft Taupe** | `#C4B5A4` | Secondary background, borders, dividers. Grounding neutral. |
| **Sage Green** | `#A3B18A` | Accent color. Buttons, highlights, botanical tie-in. |

### Secondary Colors

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| **Dusty Rose** | `#C9A9A6` | Warm accent. Floral collections, seasonal highlights. |
| **Terracotta** | `#C07A5A` | Bold accent (use sparingly). Call-to-action, sale tags. |
| **Charcoal** | `#3D3D3D` | Text color. Clean and readable without being harsh black. |
| **Antique White** | `#FAF7F2` | Light background variant. Cards, overlays. |

### Color Usage Rules
- Backgrounds are always warm — never pure white (`#FFFFFF`) or cool gray
- Text is charcoal (`#3D3D3D`), never pure black (`#000000`)
- Sage and dusty rose are accent colors — used for pops, not large areas
- Terracotta is reserved for emphasis moments only
- Overall feel: 80% neutrals, 15% sage/rose accents, 5% terracotta pops

## Typography

### Primary Typeface — Headings
**Cormorant Garamond** (Google Fonts, free)
- Weight: Medium (500) for headings, SemiBold (600) for brand name
- Usage: Headlines, product titles, brand name, section headers
- Why: Elegant serif with a classical feel that echoes the vintage art style. Refined but not stuffy.

### Secondary Typeface — Body
**DM Sans** (Google Fonts, free)
- Weight: Regular (400) for body, Medium (500) for emphasis
- Usage: Product descriptions, captions, UI text, body copy
- Why: Clean, modern sans-serif with warm geometry. Balances the serif's formality with approachability.

### Type Rules
- Headings: Cormorant Garamond, Medium, sentence case (not all caps)
- Body: DM Sans, Regular, 16px base
- Never use more than these two typefaces
- Generous line spacing (1.5–1.6) for readability
- Minimal text on graphics — let the art breathe

## Logo Usage

### Existing Logo
Stacked serif wordmark: "DELLA & DALE" in a condensed serif, arranged in a vertical block format. Visible in the Etsy shop profile.

### Usage Guidelines
- **Primary:** Stacked wordmark on warm ivory or cream backgrounds
- **Reversed:** Antique white wordmark on charcoal or sage backgrounds
- **Minimum clear space:** Half the height of the "D" on all sides
- **Minimum size:** 80px wide for digital use
- **Never:** Place on busy backgrounds, stretch, rotate, or change the typeface
- **Favicon/icon:** Use the "D&D" monogram or just the "&" from the logo as a compact mark

## Imagery Style

### Photography & Mockups
- **Styled flat lays and room mockups** showing prints framed and hung in real, lived-in spaces
- **Warm lighting** — golden hour, natural daylight. Never cool fluorescent or flash.
- **Neutral, textured backgrounds** — linen, wood, plaster walls, dried flowers. Nothing glossy or synthetic.
- **Minimal props** — a frame, a plant, a candle, a stack of books. Not cluttered.
- **Color grading** — Slightly warm, desaturated. No heavy filters. Think "morning light on cream walls."

### Product Presentation
- Show prints in frames (thin black, natural wood, or brass) in styled settings
- Include at least one clean flat mockup with no styling for clarity
- Gallery wall arrangements to show how prints work together
- Close-up details of texture and illustration quality

### Social Media Visual Style
- Clean, grid-friendly layouts with consistent warm neutral tones
- Generous whitespace — never cramped or busy
- Text overlays: minimal, Cormorant Garamond headings, DM Sans body
- Consistent border/padding treatment across posts
- Muted, cohesive feed — someone should be able to recognize a Della & Dale post without seeing the name

### What to Avoid Visually
- Pure white or cool-toned backgrounds
- Neon, saturated, or trendy color pops
- Busy patterns, gradients, or heavy drop shadows
- Stock photo aesthetic — overly staged, overly perfect rooms
- Dark/moody editing — the brand is warm and light, not dramatic
