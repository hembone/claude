# Target Audience

## Primary Persona

### "The Intentional Decorator"
**Name archetype:** Mia, 26  
**Who she is:** A young professional or creative who just moved into a new apartment and wants it to feel like *her* — not a dorm room, not a showroom, but a warm, curated space. She browses Pinterest and Instagram for home inspo, loves thrift stores and vintage finds, and gravitates toward natural, earthy aesthetics. She's budget-conscious but has taste. She'd rather have one good print on the wall than five cheap posters.

**Where she hangs out online:** Instagram (home decor and lifestyle accounts), Pinterest (mood boards, room inspo), TikTok (apartment tours, decor hauls, styling tips), Etsy (browsing for unique finds)

**What she's looking for:** Affordable art that looks expensive. Prints that feel curated and personal, not mass-produced. Easy to buy, easy to print, easy to frame.

**What wins her over:** Clean product photos showing prints styled in real rooms. A cohesive shop aesthetic that signals taste. Low prices that let her buy multiples without guilt.

## Pain Points

- **"Real art is too expensive."** Original prints and framed art from home decor stores cost $50–$200+. That's out of reach for someone furnishing a whole space.
- **"Everything on Amazon looks the same."** Mass-market wall art feels generic and impersonal. They want something that looks like a find, not a fulfillment center product.
- **"I don't know how to style my walls."** They know they want their space to look good but feel overwhelmed by gallery wall layouts, frame choices, and matching styles.
- **"I want it now."** Waiting for shipping kills impulse decorating energy. Digital downloads solve this instantly.
- **"I want my space to feel like me, not a catalog."** They're looking for personality and warmth — art that tells a story or evokes a feeling, not just fills a blank wall.

## Buying Motivations

- **Instant gratification** — Digital downloads satisfy the "I want to change my room right now" impulse
- **Affordability** — At $1.99, it's an easy yes. No buyer's remorse, no budgeting needed
- **Aesthetic identity** — Buying from a curated shop (not a marketplace search) signals taste and intention
- **Gift-giving** — Printable art bundles make easy, thoughtful last-minute gifts
- **Seasonal refreshes** — Swapping prints is the cheapest way to update a room's mood for a new season
- **Social validation** — A beautifully styled wall is inherently shareable on Instagram and TikTok
