# Voice and Tone

## Writing Style

Della & Dale writes like a knowledgeable friend with great taste — not a salesperson, not a brand account trying too hard. The voice is **calm, confident, and warm** with an undercurrent of quiet sophistication.

**Key principles:**
- **Short and intentional** — Say less. Every word should earn its place. If a caption can be three words, let it be three words.
- **Show, don't describe** — Let the art and styling do the talking. Copy supports the visual, it doesn't replace it.
- **Confident, not pushy** — We don't beg for attention or hard-sell. The work speaks for itself. We present it well and let people come to it.
- **Warm but not bubbly** — Friendly without exclamation points. Inviting without being performative.

**Example tones:**
- Product listing: "Vintage botanical illustration — soft greens, aged paper texture. Instant download, print-ready."
- Instagram caption: "The kind of print that makes a room feel like it's been yours for years."
- Pin description: "Vintage floral wall art for a warm, collected gallery wall. Digital download, $1.99."

## Tone (formal/casual, humor level, energy)

| Dimension | Setting |
|-----------|---------|
| Formality | Casual-polished. Lowercase is fine. Full sentences optional. Never stiff or corporate. |
| Humor | Rare and dry. A wry observation, never a joke or pun. |
| Energy | Calm and steady. Not excited, not flat. Think "quiet confidence." |
| Warmth | High. This brand feels like a warm room, not a cold gallery. |
| Authority | Present but understated. We know what looks good. We don't need to explain why. |

**Tone shifts by context:**
- **Product listings:** Clean, factual, minimal. Let the image lead.
- **Social captions:** Slightly more personal and evocative. A feeling, not a pitch.
- **Informational content:** Helpful and knowledgeable. Share insights without lecturing.
- **Customer service:** Genuinely warm and gracious. Thank people. Mean it.

## Vocabulary Preferences

**Words we use:**
- Curated, collected, timeless, intentional, warm
- Print, artwork, piece, find
- Gallery wall, styled, framed, hung
- Home, space, room, corner
- Simple, clean, quiet, soft

**Words we lean into for texture:**
- "Found" (as in: "a print that feels found, not bought")
- "Collected" (as in: "a wall that looks collected over years")
- "Quiet" (as in: "quiet luxury," "quiet beauty")
- "Lived-in" (as in: "a lived-in, personal space")

## Words and Phrases to Avoid

| Avoid | Why | Use instead |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| "Cute" / "adorable" / "pretty" | Too cutesy — undermines the refined tone | "Beautiful," "striking," "lovely" |
| "OMG" / "obsessed" / "slay" | Trend-chasing influencer language doesn't fit | — (just don't) |
| "Farmhouse" / "shabby chic" | Dated aesthetic associations | "Vintage," "heritage," "classical" |
| "Printable" (as primary descriptor) | Sounds cheap/craft-oriented | "Digital download," "downloadable print" |
| "Cheap" / "budget" | Undermines perceived value | "Affordable," "accessible" |
| "Limited time!" / "Don't miss out!" | High-pressure sales language | "Now available," "Just added" |
| Excessive exclamation marks | Breaks the calm, confident tone | Period. Or nothing. |
| "Shop now!" / "Link in bio!" | Generic and pushy | "Available in the shop" or just let the link exist |
| Emojis (heavy use) | Clutters the clean aesthetic | Sparingly — a single leaf or star, max |
